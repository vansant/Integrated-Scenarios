VIC Hydrology Model

The VIC model (Liang et al., 1994) is a large-scale, semi-distributed hydrologic model that simulates the hydrology in land-surface processes by incorporating variable vegetation, soil types and topography. It is semi-distributed in that it has grid cells, but also a channel network to handle surface/subsurface runoff. Like other land surface models, in VIC:
  • Grid cells are simulated independently of each other
  • Inputs are time series of daily meteorological variables(precipitation, air temperature, wind speed)
  • Water can only enter a grid cell via the atmosphere
  • Land-atmosphere fluxes and water/energy balances at the surface are simulated at daily time steps
  • Non-channel flow between grid cells is ignored. Once water reaches the channel network, it is assumed to stay in the channel (it cannot flow back into the soil).
